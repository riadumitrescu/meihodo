Zazen (Seated Meditation)

Overview

Zazen, the heart of Zen practice, is seated meditation that cultivates awareness, presence, and inner stillness. This ancient practice, literally meaning "seated Zen," emphasizes proper posture, natural breathing, and the art of simply being present without judgment or expectation.

Our zazen sessions take place in a traditional meditation hall (zendo) where you'll learn authentic techniques passed down through generations of Zen masters, creating a foundation for deeper spiritual practice and daily mindfulness.

Course Flow

  1. Arrival & Settling - Enter the meditation hall with mindful awareness
  2. Posture Instruction - Learn proper sitting position and spine alignment
  3. Breathing Awareness - Develop natural, rhythmic breathing patterns
  4. Meditation Practice - Sit in stillness with gentle guidance
  5. Walking Meditation (Kinhin) - Practice mindfulness in slow movement
  6. Return to Sitting - Deepen the practice through extended sessions
  7. Closing & Integration - Gentle transition back to daily awareness

What to Bring / Attire

  • Comfortable, loose-fitting clothing in neutral colors
  • Layers for temperature comfort during long sitting
  • Socks (shoes removed in meditation hall)
  • Personal meditation cushion if preferred (zabuton provided)
  • Water bottle for hydration breaks
  • Open mind and patience with the learning process

Notes / Safety

  • Suitable for complete beginners and experienced practitioners
  • No religious affiliation required - practice is secular
  • Alternative sitting positions available for physical limitations
  • Noble silence maintained during formal practice periods
  • Sessions begin with brief instruction for newcomers
  • Weather-independent indoor practice
  • Individual guidance available upon request
