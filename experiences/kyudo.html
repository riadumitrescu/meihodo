Kyūdō (Japanese Archery)

Overview

Kyūdō, the Way of the Bow, is a traditional Japanese martial art that combines physical precision with spiritual discipline. More than mere archery, it is meditation in motion, emphasizing proper form, breathing, and mental clarity over hitting the target.

Our kyūdō instruction follows the traditional teachings passed down through generations, focusing on the eight stages of shooting and the development of inner harmony through the practice of this ancient art.

Course Flow

  1. Preparation (Dozukuri) - Learn proper stance and posture fundamentals
  2. Bow Handling - Practice holding and positioning the traditional Japanese bow
  3. Drawing (Hikiwake) - Master the eight stages of drawing the bow
  4. Aiming (Monomi) - Develop focus and target awareness
  5. Release (Hanare) - Practice the natural release of the arrow
  6. Follow-through (Zanshin) - Maintain awareness after the shot
  7. Reflection - Analyze form and mental state for improvement

What to Bring / Attire
