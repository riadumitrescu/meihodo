Chadō (Tea Ceremony)

Overview

Chadō, the Way of Tea, is a choreographed ritual of preparing and serving Japanese green tea, accompanied by traditional sweets. This meditative practice embodies the principles of harmony (wa), respect (kei), purity (sei), and tranquility (jaku).

Our tea ceremony instruction takes place in an authentic tea house setting, where you'll learn not just the movements, but the deep philosophy and aesthetic appreciation that underlies this quintessentially Japanese art form.

Course Flow

  1. Purification Ritual - Cleanse hands and spirit before entering the tea space
  2. Appreciation - Observe and appreciate the seasonal decorations and utensils
  3. Water Preparation - Learn the proper heating and handling of water
  4. Tea Whisking - Master the technique of whisking matcha to perfect consistency
  5. Presentation - Practice the graceful movements of serving tea
  6. Mindful Consumption - Experience the meditative aspect of drinking tea
  7. Gratitude Expression - Learn traditional expressions of thanks and appreciation

What to Bring / Attire

  • Modest, comfortable clothing in subdued colors
  • Clean socks (shoes removed in tea house)
  • Ability to sit in seiza position or on cushions
  • Small hand towel (furoshiki provided if needed)
